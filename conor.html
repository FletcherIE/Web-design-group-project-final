Building Routine

Having experienced learning and training from home I can confidently say that my biggest asset was my ability to create a schedule for myself. The difficulty we all faced within lockdown was our complete breakdown of all strutcure in our life. Having worked over 10 years in the hospitality sector I felt this hit especially hard.Below I am going to give you my tips on how I built a routine and structure suited to myself and my learning journey.






Morning blitz

The most important thing to do with your day is start off on the right foot. Make your bed before anything else.That way even if you have had a not so productive day, You can return to a made bed. This is the first step in achieveing a routine. Grab yourself a nice cup of your preferred caffenined beverage and get to work on reading 7-10 pages of your favourite book. This is an excellent way to stimulate the brain,relax the mind and get the creative juices flowing all in one fell swoop. After, Do up a small task list for the day that you know you can achieve. Dont set unachievable goals! Start small (like making your bed ;)) and work up to the more arduous tasks to finish. This will instinctivly give you a sense of accomplishment and set you on a snowball affect.






Get your breaks in!

Statistics show our brains process information best in 25 minute incriments. You are not a superhuman that can process endless drones of information and retain it effectively. Use the break method! Set a subtask section in your journal for the topics you would like to revise over for the day and set break times. It dosent have to be a lon break as you dont want to drift out of focus. Take 10 minutes, Have a glass of water or catch up on your socials in this time. Then when your break ends, Get back into study mode.




Get a good nights rest

Last and definitely not least, Your sleep! Get. It. Getting a full nights sleep is paramount to your brains process of retaining all the information you have soaked in over the day.Make sure to cut your eyes off from any phones or tv's an hour before you plan to sleep. This will help you fall into a deeper sleep further enabling your brain to do its job while you rest. You've done your work. Enjoy that sleep.
